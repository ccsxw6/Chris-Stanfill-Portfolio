About Me

Christopher Stanfill (baritone) is currently pursuing a master’s degree in vocal performance at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. Chris has performed several operatic roles, some of his favorite roles performed are The Forester in The Cunning Little Vixen, The Pirate King in Pirates of Penzance, Peter in Hänsel und Gretel, Escamillio in Carmen. Chris has performed as the baritone soloist for several concert and oratorio works, including Vaughan Williams’ Fantasia on Christmas Carols; Fauré’s Requiem; Finzi’s Let Us Garlands Bring; Mozart’s Vesperae solennes de confessore, K.V. 339; Bach’s Erfreut euch, ihr Herzen, BWV 66; Handel’s Nisi Dominus, and Mozart’s Requiem. In addition to his collegiate studies, Chris has participated in Union Avenue Opera’s Crescendo program and W. Stephen Smith’s “The Naked Voice for Singers and Teachers Summer Institute.” He has participated as a young artist in the Aspen Music Festival and School, the Bach Society of St. Louis, and with Central City Opera. Chris received his Bachelor of Music Degree in Vocal Performance from the University of Missouri-St. Louis.
